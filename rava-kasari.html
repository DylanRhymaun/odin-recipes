Rava Kasari

Kesari is a pudding like sweet that is good to look at as it is to eat. To make this delicious sweet dish the rava is roasted/toasted in ghee until the fine rava granules become fragrant and change color.

  1. 1. In a heavy kadai or a thick bottomed frying pan, take 6 tablespoons ghee.
  2. 2. Heat ghee on medium-low heat till it melts completely.
  3. 3. Add 3 to 4 tablespoons cashews.
  4. 5. Fry cashews till golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and keep aside on kitchen paper towel or in a plate.
  5. 7. Stirring continuously, fry the raisins.
  6. 10. Reduce heat to low and add 1 cup (160 grams) fine rava to the ghee.
  7. 16. When you begin to roast rava, at that time, take .75 cup (190 to 200 grams) sugar in a saucepan. The sugar syrup needs to be cooked simultaneously while roasting rava.
  8. 17. Add 2 to 3 pinches of saffron strands.
  9. 18. Add 2.5 cups water. Optionally, you can also add a natural yellow or orange color extract. I added 2 drops of natural orange color extract.
  10. 23. Lower the heat and carefully pour the prepared sugar syrup in the hot ghee-rava mixture in a steady and slow stream.
  11. 28. When the mixture starts thickening, add the fried cashews and raisins, reserving a few for garnishing.
  12. 32. Turn off the heat and cover the pan for 5 minutes, just to allow the Kesari to steam.
